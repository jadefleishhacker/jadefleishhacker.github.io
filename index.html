Virtual Reality or (VR) is a computer simulated world/environment in which a person could put on a headset and controllers to be immersed in it.


Elements of Virtual Reality could be found as far as 1860s. Before technology, artists used 360˚ murals to create the illusion of VR. Then the first simulation device was made in the 1920s, as a training platform for novice pilots. In the 1957, the Sensorama was invented and then patented in 1962. This was the first device to be create an interactive experience of sound, smells, fans and stereoscopic images (image creating 3D depth illusion). From there on, Virtual Reality technology became increasingly advance, applying more realism, interactiveness and uses. VR essentially allows users to step in a world or game and interact with it. For example through VR, one could visit The Great Wall of China from all the way from California. With the headset, they would be able to “move” around and turn 360˚ to see all perspectives. Virtual Reality is commonly confused with Augmented Reality (AR), which is an interactive form that can be experience in the real world through a phone or tablet. An example of this are the Snapchat filters and Sephora’s Virtual Artist.

How It Works

Positional tracking (infrared LED) sensors pointing in all directions, locating controller’s XYZ

Thumbstick for moving around

Polarized lenses projecting 2 images, one for each eye, allowing the illusions of 3D depth of field.

Grip simulator

Trigger button

Tracking Technology, measuring the environment in real time without any external sensors/wires.

Several lenses are made to prevent passing radiation and blue light to come in contact with the user's eyes.

Foam is outlined along the head for maximum comfort and support.

USES

Virtual Reality has created many opportunities for all generations. Though it is mostly known as a platform for video games and entertainment, it has also changed the way we experience things.

It allows “hands-on” education, training for medical students, strategic training for the military, treatment for mental/psychological disorders/defects (anxiety, autism, PTSD) and many more. Over the years, Virtual Reality will become more important and relevant in our daily lives. It will become the tool of our future.

GET STARTED

Before getting a Virtual Reality headset and controllers, it is always a good idea to understand and explore the technology. This website would have briefly and

interactively explain where VR comes from, how modern technology works, how it is being used, where you can buy some, as well as providing several entertaining and educational applications.

When using VR, it is vital to choose a safe and spacious environment. It is easy to become unaware of your surroundings as your sense of

location and hearing is impaired due to the headset. Though the VR headset will locate and measure items near by, for maximum safety, choose an open space with little to no furniture and sharp edges.
